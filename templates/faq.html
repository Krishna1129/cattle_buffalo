Frequently Asked Questions

What types of images work best with the classifier?

For best results, use clear, well-lit images where the animal is clearly visible. The image should:

  • Show the entire animal (or at least most of it)
  • Be well-lit and in focus
  • Have minimal background clutter
  • Be in JPG, JPEG, or PNG format

How accurate is the classification?

Our model achieves:

  • 97% accuracy in distinguishing between cows and buffaloes
  • 89% accuracy in breed classification
  • Results are shown with confidence scores
  • For breed classification, we only proceed if the initial classification confidence is above 60%

What breeds can the system identify?

The system can identify multiple breeds including:

Cow Breeds:

  • Holstein Friesian
  • Jersey
  • Gir
  • Sahiwal
  • And many more...

Buffalo Breeds:

  • Murrah
  • Surti
  • Mehsana
  • Jaffrabadi
  • And more...

Tips for Better Results

  1. Take photos in good lighting conditions
  2. Ensure the animal is the main focus of the image
  3. Try to capture the animal's distinctive features
  4. Use high-resolution images when possible
  5. Avoid heavy image filters or editing

How can I improve accuracy?

To get the most accurate results:

  • Use multiple angles if first attempt is uncertain
  • Ensure good lighting and clear visibility
  • Check the confidence score - higher is better
  • Consider time of day and lighting conditions
  • Minimize motion blur in photos
