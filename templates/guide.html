Usage Guide

Getting Started

Welcome to the Cattle & Breed Classifier! This guide will help you get the most accurate results from our AI-powered system.

Quick Tips

  • Use clear, well-lit images
  • Ensure the animal is clearly visible
  • Avoid blurry or dark photos
  • Include the whole animal in the frame

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare Your Image

Select a clear photograph of the cattle you want to classify. The image should:

  • Be in JPG, JPEG, or PNG format
  • Show the animal clearly
  • Be taken in good lighting
  • Have minimal background distractions
2

Upload Your Image

Upload your image using one of these methods:

  1. Click the upload area to browse your files
  2. Drag and drop your image onto the upload area
3

Review Initial Classification

The system will first determine if the image contains a cow or buffalo:

  • View the classification result
  • Check the confidence score
  • Ensure confidence is above 60% for best results
4

Breed Detection

If the initial classification is successful:

  • Click "Detect Breed" button
  • Wait for breed analysis
  • View breed classification and confidence score

Understanding Results

Confidence Scores

90-100%

Very high confidence - Extremely reliable prediction

70-89%

Good confidence - Reliable prediction

60-69%

Moderate confidence - Acceptable prediction

< 60%

Low confidence - Consider retaking photo

Troubleshooting

Common Issues

Low Confidence Score

  • Try a different angle
  • Improve lighting conditions
  • Reduce background clutter
  • Ensure the animal is clearly visible

Upload Errors

  • Check file format (JPG, JPEG, PNG only)
  • Ensure file size is under 16MB
  • Try reducing image resolution
